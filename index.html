About Me

I am a mathematical biologist specializing in infectious disease modeling and ecological population dynamics. My research focuses on developing deterministic and stochastic models to assess climate-driven vector-borne disease risks and inform public health strategies. I am particularly interested in integrating environmental variables like temperature, rainfall, and photoperiod into mosquito population models.

Research

My current Ph.D. research focuses on modeling the impact of climate variability on the population dynamics of Culex mosquitoes, with applications to vector control and disease prevention. I employ ODE-based modeling, sensitivity analysis (e.g., PRCC), and climate data analysis in R and Python.

Teaching

I have served as a Teaching Assistant for undergraduate mathematics and biology courses, where I taught R programming, graded assessments, and mentored students on research projects. I have also lectured in applied mathematics and statistics at the university level.
